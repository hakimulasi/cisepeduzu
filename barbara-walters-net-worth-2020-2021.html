Early Life

Barbara Jill Walters was born in Boston, Massachusetts, on September 25, 1929, to her parents Louis “Lou” Walters and Dena Seletsky.

She went to a public school called Lawrence School in Brookline, Massachusetts. Later, she attended Ethical Culture Fieldston School and Birch Wathen School. Walter went to Sarah Lawrence College and earned her BA in English.

In 1955, Barbara Walters got married to Robert Henry Katz. Two years later, their marriage was annulled. From 1963 to 1976, she was married to Lee Guber. They have a daughter named Jacqueline Dena Guber. From 1981 to 1984, she was married to Merv Adelson. She again remarried Adelson in 1986 but divorced in 1992.

Career

After completing her college, Barbara Walters began to work for WNBT – TV, which is an affiliate of NBC. Following that, she became the producer of a children’s program called ‘Ask the Camera.’ Later, she became the producer of ‘Eloise McElhone Show.’ In 1955, she became the writer of CBS’s ‘The Morning Show.’

In 1961, she became the researcher and writer of ‘The Today Show’ on NBC. Prior to that, she has served as Redbook magazine’s writer and Tex McCrary Inc.’s publicist. She became the co-host of ‘The Today Show’ following the death of Frank McGee.

She became the co-host of ‘ABC Evening News’ from 1976 to 1978. After that, she hosted ‘20/20’ in 1979. In 1997, Walters became the co-creator, co-executive producer, and co-host of ‘The View.’ She announced her semi-retirement in 2004. She also stopped doing Oscar interviews from 2010 onwards.

Since 2014, Barbara Walters has been hosting special episodes of ‘20/20’. In 2015, she hosted the Investigation Discovery’s documentary series called ‘American Scandals.’ In the same year, she continued to host her annual ‘10 Most Fascinating People’. In 2014, she retired as the co-host of ‘The View’ and also from ABC News.

Awards & Achievements

In 1985, Barbara Walters won the Paul White Award by the Radio Television Digital News Association. She has won three Daytime Emmy Awards from 21 nominations. In 2009, she won the NAACP Image Award for Best Talk Series. She is also the winner of Women in Film Crystal + Lucy Awards in 1998. In 1989, she was inducted into the Television Hall of Fame. She has also got a Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. Additionally, she is the winner of the GLAAD Excellence in Media award.
