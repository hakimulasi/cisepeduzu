Meryl Streep Is Selling Her Tribeca Mansion For $18.3 Million

Acclaimed actor Meryl Streep is looking for a change in her New York digs. Town & Country reports that Streep is selling her Tribeca penthouse, and she's looking for a buyer willing to shell out $18.3 million. Interestingly, that price point represents a bit of a discount, compared to when Streep tried to sell the property before. In August of last year, she listed it for some $25 million, and evidently wasn't able to find a buyer.

That's not a reflection on the penthouse though, which is a luxurious waterfront spot in Tribeca, with cutting edge appliances and incredible views of New York through expansive floor-to-ceiling windows throughout. It has four bedrooms – three especially designed to accommodate guests – and five bathrooms, as well as a media room and study, all spread out across almost 4,000 square feet at the top of River Lofts tower, which is naturally outfitted with plenty of amenities of its own. As the penthouse's official listing puts it:

"The work of National Design Award–winning Tsao & McKown architects, the full-service River Lofts condominium features a recently renovated fitness center, a 24-hour doorman, a bicycle room, a garden, and an easily accessible on-site garage. From the building's elegant lobby, a keyed elevator leads directly to a private vestibule in the penthouse. It overlooks the popular Hudson River Park, and the shops, restaurants, and other attractions of TriBeCa—New York's cultural capital—and SoHo are steps away."
