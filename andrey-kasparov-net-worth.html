FAQs About Andrey Kasparov

Who is Andrey Kasparov?

Andrey Rafailovich Kasparov is an Armenian-American pianist, composer, and professor known for his significant contributions to music through performance and academic work. He holds dual citizenship in the United States and Russia.

What is he known for?

He is renowned for his work on piano duet and duo compositions, particularly his interpretations and recordings of works by Florent Schmitt. He is also acclaimed for his collaborative projects with the Invencia Piano Duo.

What awards has he won?

Among his accolades are multiple American Society of Composers, Authors, and Publishers (ASCAP) Standard Awards, prizes from the Sergei Prokofiev International Composition Competition, and recognition in the Naxos Critics’ Choice.

Where does he teach?

He is a Professor of Music at Old Dominion University in Norfolk, Virginia, where he teaches composition, piano, and music theory.

What are some notable works by him?

His discography includes several notable recordings such as Hommages Musicaux, which features works by various composers, and a series of albums dedicated to the complete piano works of Paul Bowles and Florent Schmitt.
