FAQs about Kemal Gekić

Who is Kemal Gekić?

Kemal Gekić is a Croatian-born American concert pianist and a Full Professor of Piano Performance at Florida International University in Miami, Florida.

When was he born?

He was born on February 16, 1962.

Where did he receive his early musical training?

Gekić received his early musical training from his maternal aunt, Prof. Lorenza Baturina, at the Music School J.Hatze in Split.

What notable achievement did Kemal Gekić accomplish at the age of 16?

At age 16, he entered the class of Prof. Jokuthon Mihailović at the Art Academy of the University of Novi Sad, Serbia, and graduated with the highest mark ever granted a diploma exam recital.

How did his career begin after his education?

After earning his bachelor’s degree in 1982, he was immediately given a faculty appointment by the piano department at the Art Academy of the University of Novi Sad, which he eventually directed until 1999.

What controversy surrounded Kemal Gekić in competitions?

Gekić created a sensation at the Chopin competition in Warsaw in 1985, winning audience and critics’ hearts despite jury criticism. His performances sold tens of thousands of copies in Germany and Japan.

What is one of his most recognized achievements?

One of his most recognized achievements is the landmark recording of the complete Liszt Transcendental Etudes for JVC Music Japan/USA.

Where does he teach currently?

As of the information provided, he continues to teach at Florida International University and is a guest professor at Moshashino Academy in Tokyo, Japan.

Has he been involved in any notable recordings or projects recently?

In 2019, Gekić set to tape the first book of Bach’s Well Tempered Clavier, with the recordings being unedited, all live takes.

Gekić is known for his originality, strength of interpretation, and perfect mastery of his instrument, qualities that have made him one of today’s most formidable and exciting pianists.
